# Red-Flags Checklist — 10 Wrong Assumptions

Check the audited project against each. A matched assumption is a **red flag** the
report surfaces (each maps to the dimension it damages). Red flags don't directly
set scores but explain *why* a dimension scored low and sharpen next actions.

| # | Wrong assumption | Why it fails | Better approach | Hits dim |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Users have problems, so they will search | Many feel pain but don't search — don't know the category, wording, or that a solution exists | Start from real conversations, reviews, observed workflows | 1 |
| 2 | Keyword popularity = market opportunity | Popular keywords are competitive & expensive; popularity ≠ ability to win/convert | Judge by intent + relevance + competition + conversion | 3 |
| 3 | Apple keyword popularity is precise | It's a 1–5 relative score, not exact volume or install intent | Validate with impressions, tap-through, conversion, ranking | 9 |
| 4 | SEO content should be built around keywords | Keyword-stuffed pages don't satisfy users | People-first content; keywords structure, not replace, usefulness | 7, 8 |
| 5 | Weak SERP = easy win | Authority, backlinks, freshness, depth, behavior also matter | Check difficulty, DA, content type, product-query fit | 4 |
| 6 | Post on social & users will come | Social creates attention, not high-intent traffic | Full funnel: hook → content → proof → landing → install | 5, 11 |
| 7 | Product Hunt / launch = distribution | Without an audience, a launch is weak | Build audience first; treat launch as amplification | 5 |
| 8 | Emotional/viral content = growth | Viral but irrelevant traffic doesn't convert | Use creativity to express the real problem, not entertainment | 7 |
| 9 | Directories & communities = growth | Generic directories bring low-quality traffic; communities reject spam | Communities for trust; curated niche directories as infrastructure | 6 |
| 10 | More channels = better distribution | Posting everywhere = shallow effort | Pick 1–2 channels, go deep before expanding | 5 |

## The five biggest mistakes (summary)

1. **Starting from tools instead of users** — primary evidence must come before keyword planners/SERP.
2. **Confusing search volume with purchase intent** — target where pain + fit + beatable competition + likely conversion align.
3. **Thinking content equals distribution** — content is distribution only with right audience + message + timing + repeated exposure + conversion path.
4. **Treating social media as one channel** — each platform behaves differently.
5. **Ignoring retention after installation** — the funnel continues past install to activation, repeat, review, referral.
