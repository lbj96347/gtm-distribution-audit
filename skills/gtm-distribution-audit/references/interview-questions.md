# Guided Interview — Question Bank

Use in **interview mode** when the user has no URL or brief. Ask grouped by dimension.
Stop early on any gate dimension that is clearly empty — that alone can set the
report's readiness banner. Capture answers verbatim where possible (they become
`evidence` with `source_type: "interview"`).

Keep it conversational: ask 1–3 questions at a time, not all at once. Probe for
*primary evidence* (real quotes, counts, sources), not opinions.

## Product surfaces (ask first — sets what applies)
- Where does the product actually ship? (website / web app, iOS app, Android app, Mac App Store app, direct-download desktop app, browser extension, API/CLI, a marketplace listing) → `project.surfaces`
- This decides which dimensions apply: **ASO (dim 9) and Stage 15 are skipped unless the product ships through an app store (ios/android/mac_app_store).** A website-only product is scored only on the surfaces it has, never penalized for lacking an app.

## Dim 1 — Pain evidence (GATE)
- What problem does this product solve, in one sentence?
- How do you *know* users have this problem — where did you observe it? (interviews, reviews, Reddit, support tickets, watching people work)
- How many real users/prospects have you talked to or read? Can you quote 2–3 of them?
- Do users describe a *symptom* or the *root problem*? Give an example.

## Dim 2 — User language & intent (GATE)
- What exact words do users use for this problem? (not your product words)
- How do you describe it vs how they describe it? (dev term ↔ user term)
- For your top phrases, is the user informational, comparing options, seeking a solution, or ready to buy/download?

## Dim 3 — Keyword opportunity
- What search terms do you think users type? Where did that list come from?
- Have you checked any volume/difficulty (GSC, Keyword Planner, Apple Ads 1–5)?
- Do you have long-tail, higher-intent queries — or only broad popular ones?

## Dim 4 — SERP / competition
- For your top keyword, what actually ranks today? (blog / directory / app / video / Reddit)
- Are those results weak/stale enough to beat, or authority-dominated?
- Are AI search tools already recommending certain brands for this query?

## Dim 5 — Channel selection
- Where do your users already spend time and show intent?
- Which 1–2 channels are you focusing on? Why those?
- Can you produce that channel's best content format consistently?

## Dim 6 — Directory strategy
- Are "best X" / "alternatives to X" queries common in your niche?
- Do curated lists/directories rank for them — and are they weak or strong?
- Do you have 3+ products in this niche (owned directory) or one (third-party listings)?

## Dim 7 — Content assets & format
- What content have you made? What format performs in your niche?
- Is your content pain-first or feature-first?

## Dim 8 — Landing page / on-page SEO
- Do you have a landing page? Does its headline match search intent and user language?
- Does the page deliver the same promise your content makes?

## Dim 9 — ASO (app stores only — skip entirely if no app-store surface (ios/android/mac_app_store); mark dim 9 + stage 15 na)
- What's your app title, subtitle, and keyword field? Do they use user-language keywords?
- Do screenshots lead with the problem/benefit or a feature tour?

## Dim 10 — GEO / AI visibility
- Can AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot) access your content? llms.txt?
- Is your brand mentioned in lists/sources that ChatGPT/Perplexity/AI Overviews cite?

## Dim 11 — Conversion / activation / retention
- What's your conversion path from click to install/signup?
- What is the first successful action a new user takes, and how fast?
- Do you track repeat use, reviews, referrals — or just installs?

## Dim 12 — Measurement & learning loop
- What do you measure today? Can you attribute activation back to a keyword/channel/content?
- Do you have a cadence to decide what to repeat, adjust, or drop?
